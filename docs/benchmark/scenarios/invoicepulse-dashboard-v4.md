Scenario: "InvoicePulse", a lightweight accounts receivable product for small B2B teams.

User situation:
A finance operator opens the product at the start of the day and needs to decide which customers or invoices deserve attention first.

Goal:
Design the product surface that helps the operator understand urgency, risk, expected cash impact, and the next best follow-up action.

Constraints:
- Use fictional but realistic companies, invoices, owners, payment states, dates, amounts, and activity history.
- Assume today is May 14, 2026; make due dates, overdue states, and promised payment dates internally consistent.
- Let the user narrow, prioritize, or update the work in ways that visibly change the screen.
- The experience should feel like a focused B2B operations workspace, not a marketing page or decorative analytics mockup.
- Do not mention FrontBench or benchmarking inside the generated UI.
