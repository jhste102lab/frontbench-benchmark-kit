Scenario: "Auralite", a premium wireless-headphone product page.

User situation:
A visitor is evaluating whether these headphones fit their taste, daily use, and budget, and may choose a configuration before purchasing.

Goal:
Design the product page that combines premium product storytelling with a clear path to choosing, trusting, and buying the headphones.

Constraints:
- Use fictional but realistic product details, configurations, pricing, availability, technical claims, accessories, reviews, or support promises.
- Do not imply endorsements from real people, creators, publications, or companies.
- Let the visitor explore meaningful product differences and see purchase intent or configuration changes reflected on the page.
- The page should feel like premium consumer electronics, not a SaaS dashboard, shopping mall grid, or empty startup landing page.
- Product visuals may be created with CSS shapes, gradients, material swatches, or inline SVG; do not load remote images.
- Do not mention FrontBench or benchmarking inside the generated UI.
