Scenario: "MakerCircle", a logged-in community product for local makers.

User situation:
The user is an active member with light moderator responsibilities. They want to see where to participate, what community activity is changing, and what needs attention.

Goal:
Design the community surface that balances conversation, events, people, onboarding or contribution progress, and lightweight moderation without making the page feel like an admin console.

Constraints:
- Use fictional but realistic members, posts, events, locations, skills, contribution signals, notifications, and moderation reasons.
- Assume today is May 14, 2026; make event dates and activity timestamps consistent with that date.
- Let the user search, filter, respond, follow, RSVP, review, or otherwise change visible community state in ways that fit the product.
- The experience should feel active, warm, and information-rich, not like a static marketing page or generic social feed clone.
- Do not mention FrontBench or benchmarking inside the generated UI.
