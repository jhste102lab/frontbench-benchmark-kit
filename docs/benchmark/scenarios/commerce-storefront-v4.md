Scenario: "Northstar Goods", a curated lifestyle shopping experience.

User situation:
A shopper is browsing a small curated collection and wants to find products worth buying, compare tradeoffs, and move toward checkout with confidence.

Goal:
Design the storefront surface that helps the shopper discover relevant items, understand price and availability, compare options, and see the result of purchase intent.

Constraints:
- Use fictional but realistic products, categories, prices, ratings, materials, colors, promotions, and availability states.
- Make shopping actions and their consequences visible, such as changing the shown products, selected options, comparison state, or cart state.
- The experience should feel like a polished retail product, not an admin dashboard, SaaS report, or purely editorial brand story.
- Product visuals may be created with CSS shapes, gradients, swatches, or inline SVG; do not load remote images.
- Do not mention FrontBench or benchmarking inside the generated UI.
